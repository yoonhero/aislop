# Uploaded Character Analysis

## Core Character Read

- Name working label: Jank / 짱크.
- Overall impression: dorm-life gray-set character with an awkward, deadpan, full-bodied comedic presence.
- Design priority: make the character recognizable before making the poster loud.

## Appearance Features

- Head shape: 레고 사람처럼 머리가 생김. The head reads as a rounded block: square-ish cranium, soft corners, compact jaw, and a short neck.
- Hair: thick black wavy mushroom hair. The silhouette is heavy at the top, rounded like a cap, with uneven curly clumps. It should not become sleek idol hair.
- Glasses: black 뿔테 안경. The frame is thick, round-to-oval, and visually important; it should be readable even at hoodie-back print distance.
- Face: soft cheeks, small mouth, low-energy eyes, and a slightly blank or annoyed expression. Avoid crying chibi eyes unless the joke specifically needs it.
- Body feeling: 포만한 느낌. Soft, padded, comfortable volume rather than lean/action-hero anatomy. Hoodie bulk and sweatpants width should create the shape.
- Posture: hunched, leaning, sleepy, awkward. He looks like he was caught mid-dorm-life moment.
- Clothing identity: heather-gray hoodie is the anchor. Loose gray sweatpants support the cozy set identity. Avoid black jackets, backpack straps, lab coats, white coats, school uniforms, and fantasy styling.
- Hands: relaxed, slightly limp, awkward; hands can be a humor point but should not become the identity unless doing a meme pose.
- Mood: deadpan, mildly annoyed, socially awkward, quietly funny.

## Shape Language

- Big rounded hair cap over a compact block head.
- Thick black glasses as the strongest facial graphic.
- Wide hoodie torso with soft folds and low shoulders.
- Loose pants or seated spread stance for a stable bottom-heavy silhouette.
- Small mouth and half-lidded eyes for deadpan comedy.

## What Went Wrong In The Previous Poster

- The character became too generic-anime and too handsome.
- The face lost the LEGO-block head and soft cheek identity.
- The expression became poster-neutral instead of awkward/deadpan.
- The uploaded character's gray-set, dorm-life specificity was weaker than the cloud-poster staging.

## Apparel Research Notes

- Hoodie back graphics need safe margins. Printful's safe-print guidance says important visuals and text should stay inside the safe area because printing can shift or crop near the edges.
- Back hoodie placement works best for large centered graphics because the back provides the biggest uninterrupted surface.
- Strong examples use one dominant read first, then supporting texture/details second.
- UNIQLO UT anime collaborations often succeed by using one clear hero image, limited supporting text, and a strong rectangular or collage composition.
- H&M and Superdry back-print hoodies show that a single big character/face, a compact stacked layout, or a tattoo-style centered illustration can read well on the back of a hoodie.

## Design Direction From Research

- Character first, poster second.
- Keep the character silhouette centered and simple enough to read from distance.
- Use a vertical back-print zone with safe empty edge margins.
- Use Korean headline only as manual typography after image generation.
- Prefer restrained apparel colors: gray, black, off-white, muted peach/pink, and one accent.
- Avoid a busy poster world until the character identity is locked.
