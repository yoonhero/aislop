Definitions 3
  • \(R[x]\) consists of finite formal sums a0+a1 x+...+an x^n.
  • The degree of a nonzero polynomial is the largest exponent with nonzero coefficient.
  • The evaluation map at alpha sends f(x) to f(alpha) when \(\alpha\text{ lies}\) in an extension ring.
Theorems 6

Polynomial domain theorem

If D is an integral domain, then D[x] is an integral domain.

Proof spine. The product of nonzero polynomials has leading coefficient equal to the product of leading coefficients, nonzero in D.

Division algorithm over a field

If F is a field and g(x) != 0 in \(F[x]\), then every f(x) has unique q(x),r(x) with f=qg+r and r=0 or deg r<deg g.

Proof spine. Cancel the leading term of f by multiplying g by a suitable scalar and power of x; repeat. Uniqueness follows by degree comparison.

Factor theorem

For a in F, \(f(a)=0\) iff \(x-a\) divides f(x).

Proof spine. Divide f by \(x-a\). The remainder has degree <1, so it is constant, and evaluating at a gives that constant.

R[x] is a ring

For any ring R, the set \(R[x]\) of polynomials with coefficients in R is a ring under formal addition and multiplication.

Proof spine. Coefficient formulas reduce associativity and distributivity to the same laws in R.

Evaluation homomorphism

If \(F\le E\) and alpha in E, then \(\varphi_\alpha:F[x]\to E\), \(f\mapsto f(\alpha)\), is a ring homomorphism.

Proof spine. Substitution respects finite sums and products.

Irrationality of sqrt2 via polynomials

The polynomial \(x^2-2\) has no rational zero.

Proof spine. If m/n is a rational root in lowest terms, parity forces both m and n even.

Propositions 2

Evaluation is a homomorphism

For alpha in an extension ring E of R, evaluation phi_alpha:\(R[x]\)->E preserves addition and multiplication.

Proof spine. Substitution distributes through finite sums and products.

Degree of product

If D is a domain and f,g are nonzero, deg(fg)=deg f + deg g.

Proof spine. Leading coefficients do not multiply to zero.

Examples 3

Division in Q[x]

Divide x^3+1 by x+1 to get quotient x^2-x+1 and remainder 0.

Use. factor theorem also predicts this because -1 is a root.

Failure over Z

Trying to divide x by 2 in \(\mathbb Z[x]\) fails because 2 has no inverse in Z.

Use. field coefficient condition matters.

Evaluation mod p

In \(\mathbb Z_5[x]\), f(x)=x^5-x evaluates to 0 for every element of \(\mathbb Z_5\) by Fermat.

Use. polynomial function과 polynomial 자체는 구분해야 한다.

Exercises 6

Factor test

Show \(x^2+1\) has no root in R but has roots in C.

Proof spine. Evaluate real squares; in C use i and -i.

Degree

Prove deg(f+g)<=max(deg f, deg g).

Proof spine. Leading terms may cancel.

Division

Divide \(x^4-1\) by \(x^2+1\) \(\text{over }\mathbb Q\).

Proof spine. Quotient x^2-1, remainder 0.

Polynomial domain

Why is \(\mathbb Z[x]\) an integral domain?

Proof spine. Z is an integral domain.

Evaluation kernel preview

For \(\varphi_2:\mathbb Q[x]\to\mathbb Q\), find a nonzero polynomial in the kernel.

Proof spine. Any multiple of x-2 works.

No rational root

Prove \(x^2-3\) has no rational zero by the same method as \(x^2-2\).

Proof spine. Use prime divisibility.

Proof Patterns 2
  • Division algorithm proofs are induction on degree.
  • Root/factor questions should invoke division by \(x-a\).
