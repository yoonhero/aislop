Definitions 3
  • \(U(n)\) is the group of units in \(\mathbb Z_n\).
  • Euler phi function \(\varphi(n)\) is |\(U(n)\)|, the number of residues 1<=a<=n relatively prime to n.
  • A linear congruence \(ax\equiv b\pmod m\) is an equation in Z_m.
Theorems 3

Fermat little theorem

If p is prime and p does not divide a, then \(a^{p-1}\equiv1\pmod p\).

Proof spine. The nonzero elements of \(\mathbb Z_p\) form a group of order p-1; apply Lagrange to a in that group.

Euler theorem

If \(\gcd(a,n)=1\), then \(a^{\varphi(n)}\equiv1\pmod n\).

Proof spine. The class of a is in \(U(n)\), a finite group of order \(\varphi(n)\).

Linear congruence criterion

The congruence \(ax\equiv b\pmod m\) has a solution iff \(d=\gcd(a,m)\) divides b. If it has solutions, it has d solutions modulo m.

Proof spine. Reduce by d; after division, a/d is a unit modulo m/d.

Propositions 2

Unit criterion in Z_n

The class a in \(\mathbb Z_n\) is a unit iff \(\gcd(a,n)=1\).

Proof spine. Bezout gives inverse when gcd is 1; an inverse gives a Bezout relation.

Power reduction

When \(\gcd(a,n)=1\), exponents of a modulo n may be reduced modulo the order of a, and therefore modulo \(\varphi(n)\).

Proof spine. The order of a divides \(\varphi(n)\).

Examples 3

Remainder of a large power

To compute \(7^{100}\bmod 12\), note phi(12)=4 and gcd(7,12)=1, so 7^100=(7^4)^25=1 mod 12.

Use. 지수만 줄이면 계산이 끝난다.

Solve 6x=9 mod 15

d=gcd(6,15)=3 divides 9. Divide to \(2x\equiv3\pmod5\). Since 2^{-1}=3 mod 5, x=9=4 mod 5, giving x=4,9,14 mod 15.

Use. d개의 해가 나온다.

Fermat failure condition

Fermat cannot be used for 5^k mod 10 because gcd(5,10) != 1.

Use. 공식 적용 전 unit 여부를 본다.

Exercises 4

Euler reduction

Find \(3^{100}\bmod 10\).

Proof spine. phi(10)=4 and 3^4=1 mod 10.

Congruence

Solve \(14x\equiv21\pmod{35}\).

Proof spine. d=7; reduce to \(2x\equiv3\pmod5\).

No solution

Show \(6x\equiv5\pmod{14}\) has no solution.

Proof spine. gcd(6,14)=2 does not divide 5.

Phi count

Compute phi(18).

Proof spine. Units are 1,5,7,11,13,17, so phi(18)=6.

Proof Patterns 2
  • Before using Euler, verify \(\gcd(a,n)=1\).
  • Linear congruence is unit inversion after dividing by gcd.
