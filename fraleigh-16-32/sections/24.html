Definitions 3
  • A noncommutative ring is a ring whose multiplication need not satisfy ab=ba.
  • A division ring has unity and every nonzero element is a unit, but multiplication may be noncommutative.
  • The quaternions H have basis 1,i,j,k with \(i^2=j^2=k^2=-1\) and \(ij=k=-ji\).
Theorems 5

Matrix ring theorem

For any ring R with unity, \(M_n(R)\) is a ring with unity under matrix addition and multiplication.

Proof spine. Associativity and distributivity follow from finite summation and ring distributivity in R.

Quaternion division theorem

Every nonzero quaternion has a multiplicative inverse, so H is a division ring.

Proof spine. Use conjugation: q^{-1}=conj(q)/N(q), where N(q)=q conj(q) is a positive real for q != 0.

Field distinction

Every field is a division ring, but not every division ring is a field.

Proof spine. H is the standard counterexample because ij=-ji.

Endomorphism ring

For an abelian group A, \(\operatorname{End}(A)\) is a ring under pointwise addition and composition.

Proof spine. Addition is inherited from A; multiplication is composition, and composition distributes over pointwise addition.

Wedderburn theorem

Every finite division ring is commutative; equivalently, every finite division ring is a field.

Proof spine. The proof is deeper than this study page needs, but the statement is a major boundary between finite and infinite noncommutative algebra.

Propositions 2

Order reversal for inverses

In a noncommutative ring, if a and b are units, then \((ab)^{-1}=b^{-1}a^{-1}\).

Proof spine. Multiply ab by b^{-1}a^{-1} on both sides in the correct order.

Center is commutative

The center Z(R)={a in R | ar=ra for all r in R} is a commutative subring when R has unity.

Proof spine. Elements in the center commute with everything, in particular with each other.

Examples 4

2x2 matrices

Let A=E12 and B=E21. Then AB=E11 while BA=E22.

Use. A tiny explicit witness of noncommutativity.

Quaternions

ij=k but ji=-k. Both i and j are units, but their product order matters.

Use. division does not force commutativity.

Endomorphism ring

Linear maps V->V form a ring under addition and composition. Composition is generally noncommutative.

Use. Functions and matrices share the same obstruction.

End(Z_n)

\(\operatorname{End}(\mathbb Z_n)\) is naturally isomorphic to \(\mathbb Z_n\): an endomorphism is determined by the image of 1.

Use. A concrete endomorphism ring that is commutative.

Exercises 6

Inverse order

Prove \((abc)^{-1}=c^{-1}b^{-1}a^{-1}\).

Proof spine. Multiply in order.

Center of matrices

Show the center of M_n(F) is the scalar matrices.

Proof spine. Commute with elementary matrices.

Quaternion calculation

Compute \((i+j)^2\) in H.

Proof spine. i^2+ij+ji+j^2=-2.

Bad cancellation

Find matrices A,B,C with AB=AC but B != C.

Proof spine. Use a nonzero singular A.

Endomorphism addition

For endomorphisms f,g of an abelian group A, prove f+g is again an endomorphism.

Proof spine. Use commutativity of A to rearrange f(a+b)+g(a+b).

Finite division ring consequence

Why can there be no noncommutative division ring with exactly 8 elements?

Proof spine. Wedderburn says every finite division ring is a field.

Proof Patterns 2
  • Preserve order in every multiplication line.
  • Counterexamples are easiest in \(M_2(F)\).
