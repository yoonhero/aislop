Definitions 3
  • Pairs (a,b) with a in D and b != 0 represent fractions \(a/b\).
  • \((a,b)\sim(c,d)\) iff \(ad=bc\). This is the equality rule for fractions.
  • The canonical embedding D -> Quot(D) sends a to a/1.
Theorems 4

Existence of quotient field

Every integral domain D has a field F whose elements are fractions \(a/b\) with a,b in D and b != 0, and D embeds in F.

Proof spine. Use the equivalence relation \(ad=bc\), define operations on classes, and use no zero divisors to prove well-definedness.

Minimality of quotient field

If D is embedded in a field E, then the smallest subfield of E containing D is isomorphic to Quot(D).

Proof spine. Send \(a/b\) to a b^{-1} inside E and check this is a well-defined field map.

Universal property

Any injective ring homomorphism from D to a field K extends uniquely to a field homomorphism Quot(D)->K by \(a/b\mapsto f(a)f(b)^{-1}\).

Proof spine. The formula is forced by preserving division.

Uniqueness of quotient fields

Any two fields of quotients of the same integral domain are isomorphic by an isomorphism fixing the original domain.

Proof spine. Use the universal fraction map both ways; each fraction \(a/b\) must be sent to the corresponding fraction.

Propositions 2

Fraction equality

\(a/b\)=c/d in Quot(D) iff \(ad=bc\).

Proof spine. This is the defining equivalence relation.

Nonzero inverse

If \(a/b\) != 0, then its inverse is b/a.

Proof spine. a != 0, and (\(a/b\))(b/a)=1.

Examples 3

Z to Q

Quot(Z)=Q.

Use. The general construction is exactly familiar fractions.

F[x] to rational functions

For a field F, Quot(\(F[x]\)) is F(x), the field of rational functions.

Use. polynomial fractions become legitimate elements.

Why domain is needed

\(\mathbb Z_6\) cannot be used: 2/1 would equal 0/3 by cross multiplication, while 3 is nonzero but not cancellable.

Use. zero divisors break fraction equality.

Exercises 5

Well-defined addition

Prove that \(\frac ab+\frac cd=\frac{ad+bc}{bd}\) does not depend on representatives.

Proof spine. Replace \(a/b\) by a'/b' and use ab'=a'b.

Embedding

Show \(a\mapsto a/1\) is injective.

Proof spine. a/1=0/1 implies a=0.

Rational functions

In Quot(\(\mathbb Q[x]\)), simplify (x^2-1)/(x-1).

Proof spine. It equals x+1 because x != 1 is not an evaluation condition; cancellation is polynomial factor cancellation.

Universal map

Given f:Z->R, f(n)=n, identify the induced map Q->R.

Proof spine. It is the usual inclusion of rationals into reals.

Uniqueness map

If F and K are both quotient fields of D, describe the isomorphism F->K on \(a/b\).

Proof spine. It sends \(a/b\) to the same formal fraction inside K.

Proof Patterns 2
  • For fraction constructions, every proof has a representative-choice step.
  • If a denominator might be a zero divisor, stop: quotient fields require domains.
