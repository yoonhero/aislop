Definitions 4
  • A maximal ideal M is a proper ideal with no proper ideal strictly between M and R.
  • A prime ideal P in a commutative ring satisfies ab in P -> a in P or b in P.
  • A principal ideal (a) consists of all multiples ra.
  • Prime fields are the smallest subfields: Q in characteristic 0 and \(\mathbb Z_p\) in characteristic p.
Theorems 5

Maximal quotient theorem

For a commutative ring R with unity, M is maximal iff R/M is a field.

Proof spine. If M maximal and a notin M, then M+(a)=R, so 1=m+ra and a+M has inverse r+M. Conversely, ideals between M and R correspond to ideals of R/M.

Prime quotient theorem

For a commutative ring R with unity, P is prime iff R/P is an integral domain.

Proof spine. ab in P exactly means (a+P)(b+P)=0 in R/P. Domain zero-product property is the prime condition.

Ideals in F[x]

If F is a field, every ideal of \(F[x]\) is principal.

Proof spine. Choose a nonzero polynomial of least degree in the ideal; division shows every element is its multiple.

Field ideal criterion

A commutative ring with unity is a field iff it has no proper nontrivial ideals.

Proof spine. If a nonzero a is not a unit, then (a) is a proper nontrivial ideal. Conversely, fields have only {0} and F.

Prime subfield theorem

A field has characteristic p and contains a copy of \(\mathbb Z_p\), or has characteristic 0 and contains a copy of Q.

Proof spine. Map Z into the field by n |-> n*1 and inspect the kernel.

Propositions 2

Maximal implies prime

Every maximal ideal in a commutative ring with unity is prime.

Proof spine. R/M field implies R/M domain.

Irreducible quotient

For p(x) in \(F[x]\), the quotient \(F[x]/(p)\) is a field iff p is irreducible.

Proof spine. In \(F[x]\), irreducible p generates a maximal ideal.

Examples 4

Ideals of Z

Every ideal of Z is \(n\mathbb Z\). The ideal \(n\mathbb Z\) is maximal iff n is prime, and prime iff n is prime or n=0.

Use. \(\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z\) tells the quotient story.

F[x]/(x^2+1)

Over R, (\(x^2+1\)) is maximal and the quotient is C. Over C, \(x^2+1\) factors, so the ideal is not maximal.

Use. base field matters.

Z x Z

\(\mathbb Z\times\{0\}\) is prime but not maximal? The quotient is Z, a domain but not a field.

Use. prime and maximal differ outside special rings.

Prime but not maximal

In \(\mathbb Z\times\mathbb Z\), the ideal \(\mathbb Z\times\{0\}\) is prime because the quotient is Z, but it is not maximal because Z is not a field.

Use. This separates prime from maximal.

Exercises 5

Maximal in Z

For which n is \(n\mathbb Z\) maximal in Z?

Proof spine. Exactly prime n.

Prime quotient

Show (x) is prime in \(F[x,y]\).

Proof spine. Quotient by (x) is isomorphic to F[y], a domain.

Maximal quotient

Show (\(x-a\)) is maximal in \(F[x]\).

Proof spine. Quotient is F by evaluation at a.

Not prime

Show (6) is not prime in Z.

Proof spine. 2*3 in (6), but neither 2 nor 3 is in (6).

Finite prime is maximal

Show that in a finite commutative ring with unity, every prime ideal is maximal.

Proof spine. The quotient is a finite integral domain, hence a field.

Proof Patterns 2
  • Translate ideal questions into quotient questions whenever possible.
  • Minimal-degree arguments in \(F[x]\) use division algorithm.
