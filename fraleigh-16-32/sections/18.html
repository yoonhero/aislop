Definitions 5
  • A ring R is an abelian group under +, has associative multiplication, and satisfies both distributive laws.
  • A ring with unity has a multiplicative identity 1. A commutative ring has ab=ba.
  • A unit is an element with a multiplicative inverse.
  • A field is a commutative ring with unity 1 != 0 in which every nonzero element is a unit.
  • A ring homomorphism preserves addition and multiplication; when unity matters, check whether it is required to preserve 1.
Theorems 5

Zero multiplication laws

In every ring, 0a=a0=0 and (-a)b=a(-b)=-(ab).

Proof spine. Use distributivity: 0a=(0+0)a=0a+0a, then cancel in the additive group.

Product rings

If R1,...,Rn are rings, their cartesian product is a ring under componentwise operations.

Proof spine. All axioms reduce coordinate by coordinate.

Ring isomorphism transfer

A ring isomorphism transports all purely ring-theoretic properties, such as commutativity, unity, units, and zero divisors.

Proof spine. Apply the bijective homomorphism and its inverse to the defining equations.

Subring test

A nonempty subset S of a ring R is a subring if it is closed under subtraction and multiplication.

Proof spine. Closure under subtraction gives an additive subgroup; multiplication closure supplies the inherited product.

Chinese remainder model for coprime moduli

If gcd(r,s)=1, then \(\mathbb Z_{rs}\) is isomorphic to \(\mathbb Z_r\times\mathbb Z_s\) as rings.

Proof spine. Send n mod rs to (n mod r, n mod s); Bezout gives bijectivity.

Propositions 2

Units form a group

In a ring with unity, the set of units is a group under multiplication.

Proof spine. Identity is a unit, products of units invert in reverse order, and inverses remain units.

Finite product fields are usually not fields

F x K is never a field when F and K are nonzero rings, because (1,0)(0,1)=(0,0).

Proof spine. It has zero divisors.

Examples 4

Z_n

\(\mathbb Z_n\) is a commutative ring with unity. Its units are residue classes relatively prime to n.

Use. 이후 Fermat/Euler가 이 unit group을 사용한다.

Matrix rings

\(M_n(R)\) is a ring with unity. For n>=2 it is typically noncommutative.

Use. noncommutative 반례를 찾을 때 첫 후보.

Function rings

All functions R->R form a commutative ring under pointwise addition and multiplication.

Use. ideal 예제에서 특정 점에서 0인 함수들이 다시 등장한다.

Chinese remainder example

\(\mathbb Z_{15}\) is isomorphic to \(\mathbb Z_3\times\mathbb Z_5\).

Use. This explains why arithmetic modulo a composite can split into prime-power pieces.

Exercises 6

Unit list

Find all units in \(\mathbb Z_{12}\).

Proof spine. The units are classes represented by 1,5,7,11.

Field test

Decide whether \(\mathbb Z_9\) is a field.

Proof spine. 3 is nonzero and 3*3=0 mod 9, so no.

Homomorphism check

Check whether phi:Z->\(\mathbb Z_n\), phi(m)=m mod n, is a ring homomorphism.

Proof spine. Verify both operations. Kernel will be \(n\mathbb Z\) later.

Matrix noncommutativity

Give two 2x2 matrices over R with AB != BA.

Proof spine. Elementary matrices E12 and E21 work.

Subring test

Use the subring test to show \(2\mathbb Z\) is a subring of Z without unity.

Proof spine. It is closed under subtraction and multiplication but does not contain 1.

CRT calculation

Find the element of \(\mathbb Z_{15}\) corresponding to (1,2) in \(\mathbb Z_3\times\mathbb Z_5\).

Proof spine. Solve n=1 mod 3 and n=2 mod 5.

Proof Patterns 2
  • When proving ring identities, use additive cancellation; do not divide unless a unit is known.
  • When disproving field, exhibit one nonzero nonunit or one zero divisor.
